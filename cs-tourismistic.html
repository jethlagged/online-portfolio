Tourismistic

A concept design I thought of for a tourism website. I originally designed this for a contest, but someone else had the winning design. In retrospect, there were many things I would have done differently (both in UI/UX Design) but I had quite a lot of fun working on this and I got to learn a lot while designing it, so I thought I'd add it to my portfolio.

Year

2018

Type

Marketing website

The challenge

When I first saw this project brief, the first thing I noticed that there was an overwhelming amount of information the client wanted to display. 2018 Designer Jeth would have made sure to do everything the client wanted and, as current me has learned, isn't the smartest way to design.

But just so we're clear, 2018 Designer Jeth did try to put all the information in one homepage. And as expected, it could have been better.

The solution

Since time was of the essence (I only had about 4 days to conceptualize and design the whole website), I had to make sure that I was prioritizng the right parts to design. I imagined being in the place of a potential user and where I would expect certain kind of information to be and what would reel me in and keep me on the website.

And in less than a week's time, this version of Tourismistic was born.
